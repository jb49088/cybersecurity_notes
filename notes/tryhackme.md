# TryHackMe

TryHackMe is a beginner-friendly online cybersecurity learning platform that provides guided, browser-based learning rooms covering a wide range of security topics. It is widely recommended as a starting point for those new to cybersecurity due to its structured learning paths, in-browser attack machines, and step-by-step instructional format.

- **Learning rooms:** TryHackMe's primary content format is the room, a self-contained module that combines reading material, guided questions, and hands-on tasks within a single interface. Rooms range from introductory topics like networking fundamentals to more advanced subjects like Active Directory attacks and malware analysis.
- **Learning paths:** TryHackMe organizes rooms into structured learning paths such as Pre-Security, SOC Level 1, and Jr Penetration Tester, providing a clear progression for learners who are unsure where to start or how to structure their studies.
- **Browser-based environment:** TryHackMe provides an in-browser attack machine and target environments, meaning learners can begin practicing immediately without needing to configure a local virtual machine setup, significantly lowering the barrier to entry.
- **Guided vs free roam:** Unlike HackTheBox, which largely drops users into machines without guidance, TryHackMe explicitly walks learners through concepts and tasks with hints and explanations, making it more suitable for complete beginners building foundational knowledge.
- **Comparison to HackTheBox:** TryHackMe and HackTheBox serve complementary roles in a learner's development. TryHackMe is generally the recommended starting point for building foundational knowledge, while HackTheBox is more appropriate once a learner is comfortable with core concepts and ready for less guided, more realistic challenges.

TryHackMe is one of the most accessible entry points into practical cybersecurity learning, offering a structured and supportive environment that builds the foundational skills needed to progress toward more advanced platforms, certifications, and real-world security work.
