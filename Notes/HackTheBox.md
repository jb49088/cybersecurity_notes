# HackTheBox

HackTheBox (HTB) is an online cybersecurity training platform that provides a large library of intentionally vulnerable machines, challenges, and labs designed to develop practical offensive and defensive security skills. It is widely regarded as one of the most respected platforms in the security community, with active use by beginners, experienced professionals, and hiring managers who use HTB rankings as a signal of technical ability.

- **Machines:** The core offering of HackTheBox is its library of vulnerable virtual machines, each simulating a realistic target with one or more vulnerabilities that must be chained together to achieve full compromise. Each machine has two flags, a user flag obtained upon gaining initial access and a root flag obtained after privilege escalation.
- **Difficulty ratings:** Machines are rated by difficulty from easy to insane, allowing learners to progress at an appropriate pace. Easy and medium machines are most relevant for beginners and those preparing for certifications or bug bounty hunting, while hard and insane machines demand deep technical knowledge and creative thinking.
- **Active vs retired machines:** Active machines have no publicly available solutions, encouraging independent problem solving. Retired machines have community-written writeups and official walkthroughs available, making them valuable learning resources for understanding approaches and techniques after attempting challenges independently.
- **Pro Labs and Battlegrounds:** HackTheBox offers Pro Labs, which are full simulated enterprise network environments designed to replicate real-world penetration testing engagements, and Battlegrounds, a competitive real-time attack and defense mode where players compete against each other on live machines.
- **CTF challenges:** Alongside machines, HackTheBox hosts a large library of standalone CTF-style challenges across categories including web, cryptography, reverse engineering, forensics, and binary exploitation, providing focused skill development outside of full machine compromises.
- **Community and reputation:** HackTheBox has a strong community and is frequently referenced by security employers as a meaningful indicator of hands-on ability, making it a valuable addition to a portfolio when pursuing roles in penetration testing or bug bounty hunting.

HackTheBox is one of the most effective platforms for developing the practical skills required for offensive security work, offering a progression path from foundational concepts through to advanced enterprise-level attack simulations.
