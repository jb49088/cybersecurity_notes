# Capture the flag (CTF)

Capture the flag (CTF) is a type of cybersecurity competition where participants solve security-themed challenges to find hidden strings of text called flags, which are submitted to a scoring system for points. CTFs are one of the most effective and widely used methods for developing practical offensive and defensive security skills in a legal and controlled environment.

- **Flag format:** Flags are typically formatted strings that follow a competition-specific pattern, such as CTF{s0me_s3cr3t_string}, hidden within challenges and retrieved by successfully exploiting a vulnerability, solving a puzzle, or reversing a program.
- **Competition formats:** CTFs are primarily run in two formats. Jeopardy-style CTFs present participants with a board of independent challenges across multiple categories, where each solved challenge awards points based on difficulty. Attack-defense CTFs pit teams against each other where each team maintains their own vulnerable services while simultaneously attacking opponents.
- **Challenge categories:** CTF challenges are typically organized into categories including web exploitation, binary exploitation and pwn, reverse engineering, cryptography, forensics, steganography, and OSINT. Each category develops a distinct set of skills relevant to real-world security work.
- **Relevance to bug bounty:** CTFs are an excellent stepping stone toward bug bounty hunting, as web exploitation categories in particular closely mirror the vulnerability classes encountered in real programs, including SQL injection, cross-site scripting, CSRF, path traversal, and authentication bypass.
- **Learning resources:** CTF writeups, where participants document their solution process after a competition ends, are among the most valuable free learning resources in security. Reading writeups for challenges you could not solve exposes you to new techniques and tools in a practical context.
- **Platforms:** Platforms such as HackTheBox, TryHackMe, and PicoCTF provide accessible CTF-style challenges for beginners and experienced players alike, offering a structured environment to develop skills at your own pace outside of timed competitions.
- **CTFtime:** CTFtime is the central hub for the CTF community, listing upcoming competitions, archiving past events, and maintaining team and player rankings, making it the first place to look when searching for competitions to enter.

CTFs are widely regarded as one of the best ways to build the hands-on technical skills required for a career in offensive security, providing a legal and structured environment to practice exploitation techniques that directly translate to penetration testing and bug bounty hunting.
